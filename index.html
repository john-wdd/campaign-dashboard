🏆
Win #1 — Explosive Day-2 Surge
Between Feb 19 and Feb 20, total views jumped from 3.19M to 10.22M — a +220% single-day increase across all platforms. This signals strong algorithmic pickup, likely triggered by early engagement velocity. The content hit critical distribution thresholds on IG and TT simultaneously.
📈
Win #2 — TikTok Overtakes Instagram
TikTok started behind IG on Day 1 (693K vs 940K) but surpassed it by Feb 24 (9.57M vs 6.74M) and extended its lead to 20.3M vs 10.1M by Feb 28. TT's algorithm continued pushing the video far longer than IG, indicating excellent hook/completion rate performance.
💎
Win #3 — Instagram Engagement Quality
Despite IG having fewer views than FB and TT, it consistently produces the highest engagement rate (3.44% on Feb 28 vs FB's 0.41%). Instagram's audience is more intentional — they're not just scrolling past. This is your highest-quality audience segment.
⚠️
Red Flag #1 — YouTube Missing Final-Day Data
The Feb 28 YouTube row has NULL values for both views and engagement. This creates an incomplete cumulative total and distorts the "All Platforms" figure. Actual total views are likely higher than 50.4M. Needs to be corrected before sharing externally — don't present incomplete data to a client.
📉
Red Flag #2 — Facebook Engagement Collapse
Facebook has the highest view count (19.95M) but the lowest engagement rate of any platform with complete data — just 0.41% by Feb 28, down from 2.67% on Day 1. While some dilution is expected at scale, this drop suggests FB's audience is largely passive. Views are being served but not acted upon.
🐢
Flag #3 — Growth Deceleration Starting Feb 24
After the massive Day 2 spike (+220%), growth rates have been steadily declining: +32%, +29%, +13%, +16%, +18%, +16%. This is normal content decay, but it's important to note the curve is flattening. Without a boost (repost, collaboration, paid push), the video is approaching its organic ceiling.
Executive Summary

The "Meen Yadob" campaign launched on February 19, 2026 and achieved viral scale within 48 hours, accumulating over 126.5 million cumulative views across Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and TikTok by March 8 — an 18-day window. The sharpest growth occurred on Day 2, when total views tripled from 3.2M to 10.2M, signaling strong algorithmic amplification across platforms simultaneously. TikTok leads all platforms with 46.60M views — now virtually neck-and-neck with the 2025 Kol Sana campaign's 46.74M TikTok total, despite still growing. Facebook is the second-highest volume driver (42.93M) but shows the weakest audience quality at 0.25% engagement rate — reach without resonance. Instagram delivers the highest engagement quality at 1.69% on 25.22M views, making it the most valuable platform for audience depth. YouTube holds steady at 11.75M views with a 0.62% engagement rate. Growth is stabilizing at 5–6% day-over-day as the campaign enters its long tail, while total engagements have reached 844K — more than 4× the 2025 Kol Sana campaign.
